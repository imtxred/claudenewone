# Psychological Triggers Reference

## Trigger Categories Overview

| Category | Core Mechanism | When to Use |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Fear-Based** | Loss aversion, threat response | Problem-aware audiences, urgency |
| **Desire-Based** | Aspiration, status, pleasure | Solution-aware, lifestyle products |
| **Social** | Conformity, authority, belonging | Product-aware, building trust |
| **Cognitive** | Information gaps, anchoring | Hooks, pricing, comparisons |
| **Pain/Pleasure** | Immediate relief vs investment | Depends on product type |
| **Identity** | Who they are/want to be | Tribe products, transformation |

---

## Category 1: FEAR-BASED TRIGGERS

### 1.1 Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)
**Mechanism:** People fear missing opportunities more than they value having them.
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware → Most Aware
**Best for:** Limited offers, launches, trending products

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Only 3 left in stock" | E-commerce |
| "1,847 people viewing this now" | Travel/Booking |
| "Sale ends in 2 hours" | Any limited offer |
| "Everyone's talking about this except you" | Trending |
| "Your competitors are already using this" | B2B SaaS |

### 1.2 Fear of Being Left Behind (FOBL)
**Mechanism:** Fear of falling behind peers, progress, trends.
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware
**Best for:** Tech, education, career tools, investing

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "While you do it manually, competition automated this months ago" | Automation |
| "The skills that got you here won't get you there" | Education |
| "Your industry is changing. Are you?" | Professional dev |

### 1.3 Fear of Loss (Loss Aversion)
**Mechanism:** Losses feel 2-2.5x stronger than equivalent gains.
**Awareness level:** Unaware → Problem Aware
**Best for:** Insurance, security, savings, protection

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "You're losing $340/month without knowing it" | Finance apps |
| "Every day without this, you fall further behind" | Productivity |
| "Your data is exposed right now" | Security |

### 1.4 Fear of Failure
**Mechanism:** Fear of public failure or not meeting expectations.
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware
**Best for:** Education, parenting, professional tools

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Don't be the parent who didn't know" | Parenting apps |
| "Your presentation could make or break this deal" | Presentation tools |
| "What if they find out you don't know what you're doing?" | Professional courses |

### 1.5 Fear of Judgment
**Mechanism:** Fear of negative evaluation from others.
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware
**Best for:** Beauty, fashion, social skills

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "What are people thinking when they see your skin?" | Skincare |
| "Stop wondering if people judge your outfit" | Fashion |
| "Never feel embarrassed ordering wine again" | Education |

### 1.6 Fear of Regret
**Mechanism:** Anticipating future regret about inaction.
**Awareness level:** Product Aware → Most Aware
**Best for:** Big decisions, once-in-lifetime opportunities

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "In 5 years, you'll wish you started today" | Investment/Education |
| "Don't let this be another thing you meant to do" | Health/Fitness |
| "Your future self will thank you" | Long-term benefits |

## Category 2: DESIRE-BASED TRIGGERS

**Mechanism:** Desire to look better in others' eyes, raise social status.
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware → Product Aware
**Best for:** Luxury, beauty, fashion, visible products

| Example | Product Type |

| "Turn heads everywhere you go" | Fashion/Beauty |
| "The watch successful people notice" | Luxury |
| "Finally feel confident at the beach" | Fitness |
| "Make them wonder what changed" | Transformation |

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "By invitation only" | Exclusive memberships |
| "Not for everyone" | Premium products |
| "Only 100 ever made" | Limited editions |
| "The waitlist is 6 months. Skip it today." | High-demand |

| "Welcome to the family" | Brand loyalty |
| "Finally, your people" | Niche communities |
| "You're one of us now" | Identity products |

### 2.4 Power / Control
**Mechanism:** Desire to control one's life and circumstances.
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware
**Best for:** Productivity, finance, health tracking

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Take back control of your mornings" | Productivity |
| "Own your schedule" | Time management |
| "Never be surprised by a bill again" | Finance apps |
| "You decide when, where, how" | Flexibility products |

### 2.5 Achievement / Mastery
**Mechanism:** Desire for competence and skill mastery.
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware
**Best for:** Education, skills, games, fitness

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Go from zero to fluent in 90 days" | Language apps |
| "Master Excel in one weekend" | Skill courses |
| "Level up your [skill]" | Education |
| "Become the person who..." | Transformation |

### 2.6 Freedom / Independence
**Mechanism:** Desire for autonomy and freedom from constraints.
**Awareness level:** Unaware → Problem Aware
**Best for:** Remote work, financial products, travel

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Work from anywhere" | Remote tools |

### 2.7 Convenience / Ease
**Mechanism:** Desire to simplify life and reduce friction.
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware → Product Aware
**Best for:** Automation, delivery, time-saving

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Done for you" | Services |
| "Skip the hassle" | Convenience |

## Category 3: SOCIAL TRIGGERS

### 3.1 Social Proof
**Mechanism:** Looking to others' behavior for validation.
**Awareness level:** Product Aware → Most Aware
**Best for:** Any product, especially high-consideration

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "10 million customers can't be wrong" | Mass-market |
| "Rated #1 by dermatologists" | Professional endorsement |
| "4.9 stars from 50,000 reviews" | E-commerce |
| "As seen on [authority]" | Media mentions |

### 3.2 Authority
**Mechanism:** Trust in experts and authoritative sources.
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware → Product Aware
**Best for:** Health, finance, technical products

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Developed by Stanford researchers" | Science-backed |
| "Doctor-recommended" | Health |
| "Used by Fortune 500 companies" | B2B |

### 3.3 Reciprocity
**Mechanism:** Feeling obligated after receiving something free.
**Awareness level:** Unaware → Problem Aware
**Best for:** Lead magnets, free trials

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Free guide: How to..." | Lead magnets |
| "Here's $10 to try us" | Credits/Coupons |
| "No strings attached" | Free trials |

### 3.4 Liking / Relatability
**Mechanism:** Buying from people who are similar or likeable.
**Awareness level:** All levels
**Best for:** Personal brands, UGC, testimonials

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "I was just like you..." | Transformation stories |
| "As a busy mom, I get it" | Relatability |
| "Built by [people like target]" | Founder stories |

### 3.5 Consensus
**Mechanism:** Following what many others are doing.
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware → Product Aware

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "America's #1 selling [category]" | Market leaders |
| "Why everyone's switching to..." | Competitive positioning |
| "The [product] going viral right now" | Trending |

## Category 4: COGNITIVE TRIGGERS

### 4.1 Curiosity Gap
**Mechanism:** Brain can't tolerate incomplete information.
**Awareness level:** Unaware → Problem Aware
**Best for:** Headlines, hooks, email subjects

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "The one thing dermatologists won't tell you" | Skincare |
| "What happens when you stop [common habit]" | Health |
| "The mistake 90% of [audience] make" | Education |
| "The secret ingredient is..." | Any product |

### 4.2 Contrast / Comparison
**Mechanism:** Perception depends on comparison context.
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware → Product Aware
**Best for:** Pricing, before/after, competitive positioning

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Half the price, twice the quality" | Value positioning |
| "What I spent vs what I saved" | Finance |
| "The hard way vs the easy way" | Solutions |

### 4.3 Anchoring
**Mechanism:** First information anchors all subsequent judgment.
**Awareness level:** Product Aware → Most Aware
**Best for:** Pricing, value propositions

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Normally $200, today $49" | Discounts |
| "A $5,000 value for just $97" | Courses |
| "Costs less than your daily coffee" | Subscriptions |

### 4.4 Commitment / Consistency
**Mechanism:** Desire to be consistent with past actions.
**Awareness level:** Product Aware → Most Aware
**Best for:** Upsells, subscriptions, retention

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "You've already started. Don't stop now." | Continuation |
| "Since you loved X, you'll love Y" | Cross-sells |
| "Continue your journey" | Subscriptions |

### 4.5 Sunk Cost
**Mechanism:** Reluctance to "waste" invested effort.
**Awareness level:** Most Aware (existing customers)
**Best for:** Retention, upgrades, renewals

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Don't lose your progress" | Renewals |
| "Keep your streak alive" | Gamification |
| "You've come so far" | Milestone messaging |

## Category 5: PAIN/PLEASURE TRIGGERS

### 5.1 Immediate Pain Relief
**Mechanism:** People pay faster to stop pain than to gain pleasure.
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware
**Best for:** Pain-point products, urgent solutions

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Stop the bleeding" | Urgent solutions |
| "End [pain] tonight" | Fast relief |
| "Never deal with [problem] again" | Permanent solutions |

### 5.2 Delayed Gratification / Investment
**Mechanism:** Promise of future reward for action today.
**Awareness level:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware
**Best for:** Education, fitness, investment

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "In 90 days, you won't recognize yourself" | Transformation |
| "Plant the seed today, harvest tomorrow" | Investment |
| "Your future self is counting on you" | Long-term |

### 5.3 Instant Gratification
**Mechanism:** Desire for immediate results without effort.
**Awareness level:** Solution Aware → Most Aware
**Best for:** Fast solutions, impulse products

| Example | Product Type |
|---------|-------------|
| "Results in 24 hours" | Fast-acting |
| "Instant access" | Digital products |
| "See the difference immediately" | Beauty/Skincare |

## Trigger Selection by Awareness Level

| Awareness | Primary Triggers | Secondary |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Unaware** | Curiosity, Fear of Loss, Reciprocity | Problem revelation |
| **Problem Aware** | Pain Relief, Fear of Failure, Relatability | Identity, Achievement |
| **Solution Aware** | Contrast, Authority, Social Proof | Exclusivity, Power |
| **Product Aware** | Consensus, Commitment, Anchoring | Scarcity, Belonging |
| **Most Aware** | FOMO, Urgency, Sunk Cost | Instant Gratification |

---

## Effective Trigger Combinations (Stacking)

| Combination | Example | Why It Works |
|-------------|---------|--------------|
| **FOMO + Social Proof** | "47 people bought in the last hour" | Urgency validated by others |
| **Authority + Fear** | "Doctors warn: ignoring this could cost you" | Expert-backed fear |
| **Curiosity + Contrast** | "The $5 secret that replaced my $200 routine" | Mystery + value |
| **Identity + Exclusivity** | "Not for beginners. For serious [identity] only" | Aspirational filtering |
| **Pain + Instant Relief** | "Stop the itch in 30 seconds" | Problem + immediate solution |
| **Reciprocity + Commitment** | "Here's free guide. Now let me show next step" | Give first, then ask |
